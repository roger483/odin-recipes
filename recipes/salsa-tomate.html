Salsa de Tomate

What is salsa de tomate?

Salsa de tomate is a staple in Mexican cuisine because it is a very versatile ingredient that can be added to practically most dishes that need some spice or simply eaten by dipping your corn chip of choice into. Not only does it taste much better than store-bought salsa but grilling the ingredients will add to the already full-bodied flavor of the salsa

Ingredients:

  • 7 tomatoes
  • 4 tomatillos
  • 10 chiles serranos
  • 1/4 onion
  • 2 garlic bulbs

Steps:

  1. Grill your veggies

    2. So, this recipe is fairly straight forward, as grilling the veggies is going to be the main step in making the salsa. Grill the tomatoes until the skin starts to get nice and toasted and starts to slide off. Everything else will follow a smilar path but just grill them until most sides are nice and charred. The charred skin will add a nice smoky flavor to the salsa.

  2. Mash everything together or blend it

    3. This is where a molcajete will come in handy if you have one, otherwise you can use your hands or mash everything inside a plastic container using the bottom of a cup. The point will be that you'll be mashing everything until you get the consistency of salsa and how much mashing you do will depend on how chunky you want the sauce to be. Add salt while mixing. My mom likes to just throw everything together and the blender is usually pretty good about giving the correct consistency.

  3. Enjoy!

    4. You can enjoy your salsa de tomate as a dip or add it to traditional food to make it taste even better and give it some good spice.
