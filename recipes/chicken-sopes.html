Chicken Deep-fried Sopes Recipe

What Are Chicken Sopes?

Chicken sopes, or "sopes de pollo" in Spanish, are a type of dish that consist of a round, fried puck of maiz similar to a tortilla but thicker and smaller in diameter than a tortilla, that is usually topped with a protein and a sauce. Common toppings are chicken, beef, and refried beans, which are then covered with a guacamole sauce or sour cream (or both! yum!) and finished with cheese, lettuce, radish slices, or onions. The best thing about sopes is that you get to choose the various toppings you want and there's no right way to make them. If served as an appetizer, one or two will suffice but if they are the main dish, four would complete a full dish. Our recipe wil only be using a few ingredients but you can add almost any combination of toppings to yours.

Ingredients:

  • 2 avocados
  • 3 tablespoons of mexican sour cream
  • 1 can of pickled peppers
  • 4 chicken breasts
  • 1 white onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • Cheese (specifically, queso fresco)
  • Maseca
  • Flour

Optional toppings:

  • Sliced radishes
  • Shredded lettuce
  • Sliced onions
  • Refried beans
  • Sour cream

Steps

  1. Make the chicken

    2. You'll want to start with making the chicken by throwing it in an oiled pan. Cook for 25-30 minutes on medium-high. When it is done cooking, take it out and shred the chicken, then, put it back in the pan with the onion, tomato, salt, pepper to taste, and a the garlic.

  2. Make the sopes

    3. Optional: you can purchase the sopes pre-made from a Mexican supermarket but they are better made from scratch!

    To make the sopes from scratch you will have to procure nixtamalized corn flour like Maseca or Torti Masa. Get a large bowl and put the corn flour, a bit of regular flour, and a pinch of salt in. Then, begin adding a bit of water slowly to the corn flour and mix for a couple of minutes until you get a nice texture that doesn't stick to your hands much and should resemble play dough.

    Next, take bit of dough and roll it into a ball. Then, with water-moistened hands, flatten the ball into a patty. Generally, you don't want the patty to be too thick or too thin. Keep in mind that you'll be biting into the thickness of the sope, plus all of the other ingredients stacked on each other. You'll want manageable bites. Slap the patty from one hand to another until you have a nice disk with a 4-inch diameter. Add more corn flour as necessary.

    Lastly, there are a things you can do to personalize the sopes to your liking. You can either cook the dough on a griddle for healthier meal, or you can deep-fry the sope for a nice crispy texture. If cooking on a griddle, cook each side for about a minute each and if frying, fry the dough until golden brown. Since we don't make them too often in my household, we usually like to deep-fry them.

  3. Make the guac sauce

    4. Making the guac sauce we use at home is fairly simple. You will need to get two avocados, three tablespoons of sour cream, and a can of pickled jalapenos. You'll want to buy the pickled peppers that come chopped already, or simply de-vein them and take off the stems. One or two peppers will suffice, adjust for preference. Grab all your igredients, plus about a quarter cup of the pickled pepper juice, and throw them in a blender until you get a nice creamy texture.

  4. Put it all together

    5. Here's the fun part in making sopes; you can customize them however you like. My preferred way to eat them is by putting the chicken on the sope, covering that in guac sauce, then topping it off with some queso fresco but as I mentioned before, you can top them with whatever other Mexican-styled ingredients you may like.

  5. Enjoy!!
